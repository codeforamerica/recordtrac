About NYC Open Gov

NYC Open Gov is a quick, simple way for you to submit a public records request to the Your agency name.

Most information on this site is public. Your contact information, and personal information that may be contained in government documetns will not be made available to the public.

What is NYC Open Gov?

NYC Open Gov is a quick, simple way for individuals to submit Freedom of Information Law (FOIL) requests to Your agency name. The web application also allows government employees manage, respond to, and fulfill incoming requests. By displaying all records requests and responsive documents, members of the public may find what they need without having to create a new public record request.

Why aren't all documents uploaded on NYC Open Gov?

The Your agency name will not publicly release records or information that violates your right to privacy. The Your agency name will not make available records that have been exempted from disclosure by Federal, State, or Local statute, including records that would impair contract awards or collective bargainaing negotiations, are trade secrets, are compiled for law enforcement services and if disclosed would interfere with investigations or judicial proceedings, deprive a person to a fair trial, identify a confidential source, reveal investigative techniques, or endanger a person's life.

History of NYC Open Gov (Previously RecordTrac)

The 2013 Code for America fellows created RecordTrac with the City of Oakland. As public records requests became larger and more complex, city employees found coordinating a response to be difficult. The old software system no longer met their needs and many requests remained untracked.

Members of the public complained they didn’t understand what happened after they submitted their requests and it took too long to get the records they needed.

By making everything completely transparent, you can know your request is fulfilled.
